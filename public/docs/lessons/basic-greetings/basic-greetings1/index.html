    Exercises #

    Let’s put those greetings into practice with a few fun exercises!

    1. Multiple Choice #

    Choose the correct Italian greeting for each situation.

    1. You meet a friend in the morning. What do you say?

      • A) Buonasera
      • B) Ciao
      • C) Buonanotte
      • Answer: B) Ciao

    2. It’s 8 PM, and you want to greet someone politely. What do you say?

      • A) Buongiorno
      • B) Salve
      • C) Buonasera
      • Answer: C) Buonasera

    3. You’re going to bed and want to wish someone goodnight. What do you say?

      • A) Buonanotte
      • B) Ciao
      • C) Buongiorno
      • Answer: A) Buonanotte

    2. Fill in the Blanks #

    Fill in the blank with the appropriate Italian greeting.

    1. It’s 11 AM, and you see your neighbor. You say, “_____.”

      • Answer: Buongiorno

    2. You’re leaving a group of friends after a fun evening. You say, “_____.”

      • Answer: Ciao or Buonasera

    3. You’re greeting a new person in a polite, neutral way. You say, “_____.”

      • Answer: Salve

    3. Practice Dialogue #

    Read and complete the dialogue below using the correct greetings.

    Scenario: You’re at an Italian café, meeting the barista in the morning.

    • You: (say hello politely in the morning)
    • Barista: Buongiorno! Come stai?
    • You: Bene, grazie! (say “goodbye” informally)

    Answers:

    • You: Buongiorno!
    • You (at the end): Ciao!

    4. Creative Exercise: Write Your Own Dialogue #

    Imagine meeting an Italian friend in the afternoon and having a brief exchange. Write your own dialogue using at least two greetings. Here’s an example to get you started:

    Example:

    • You: Ciao!
    • Friend: Ciao! Come va?
    • You: Tutto bene, grazie. Buonasera!
    • Friend: Buonasera!

    This mix of exercises will reinforce the greetings learned in Lesson 1 and allow learners to apply the words in different contexts. You can create a separate lesson1-exercises.md file if you’d prefer to keep the lesson and exercises distinct, and link to it from lesson1.md. Let me know if you’d like to adjust or add any other types of exercises!
